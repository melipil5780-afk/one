Why EMDR Works

Trauma is not stored as a story—it is stored as sensation, emotion, and survival response. EMDR works directly with the brain’s processing systems, allowing experiences to resolve without forcing re-telling or re-living.

The Work

Phase One

We identify nervous-system patterns, attachment wounds, and core memory targets while ensuring safety and pacing.

Phase Two

EMDR Processing

Bilateral stimulation allows memories to reprocess, reducing emotional charge while restoring adaptive meaning.

Phase Three

Integration

We consolidate change—supporting embodiment, identity repair, and a sustained sense of internal safety.
